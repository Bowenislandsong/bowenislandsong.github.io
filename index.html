Research Interests

Focus Areas: Machine Learning ¬∑ AI for Health ¬∑ Explainable AI ¬∑ Real-Time (Low Latency) Inference ¬∑ Multi-Modal Sensor Data ¬∑ Distributed Systems ¬∑ Cloud & Datacenter Efficiency ¬∑ Privacy-Preserving AI

My work develops interpretable machine learning methods that run efficiently on everyday devices. A key focus is using wearable sensor data‚Äîsuch as inertial measurement units (IMUs)‚Äîto estimate important biomechanical signals like ground reaction forces, joint moments, and joint angles. These measurements are vital for monitoring athlete performance, preventing injuries, and supporting applications in rehabilitation and robotics.

Recently, I introduced a lightweight approach called SVD Embedding Regression (SER), which makes accurate predictions from IMU data with very low computational cost. Building on this, I developed a Federated SER framework that allows models to be trained across multiple athletes or institutions without sharing raw data‚Äîpreserving privacy while improving accuracy through collaboration. This work helps create scalable, trustworthy AI systems for health monitoring outside of the lab.

Beyond health applications, I specialize in cloud and distributed systems. My prior research has applied queueing theory to optimize datacenter capacity and developed bandwidth-efficient solutions for large-scale data synchronization. These experiences connect my interests in efficient computing with real-world health applications.
